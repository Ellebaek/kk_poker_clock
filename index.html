Rules:

Standard Texas Hold'em game rules (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Texas_hold_em#Rules) and a few club specific rules apply. Listed below is a mix of club specific rules and a few of the most commonly discussed rules.

  • Cards and chips must be visible on the table at all times. Large chips should be placed in front.
  • Players have the right to request your chip count at any time.
  • If the dealer accidentally flips over a card while dealing hole cards, it is considered a misdeal. If the dealer accidentally flips a card later in the hand, that card will be burned face up.
  • No string bets are allowed. Announce that you are betting or raising before doing so. Any other action will be considered a call.
  • No player may miss their big blind, and no one is required to pay the small blind twice in a row. If the small blind busts out, then the blinds post their money as usual, and the button remains in place. If the big blind busts out, the next big blind posts their money as usual, and there is a dead small blind.
  • The minimum bet or raise is equal to the big blind. Any re-raise must be equal to or greater than the previous raise.
  • When the game is heads-up, the small blind is the button.
  • If two players bust on the same hand (multiple busts), the player with the greater chip count at the start of the hand finishes in the higher position.

Etiquette:

  • Don't splash the pot. People should be able to see the number of chips you bet or call.
  • Commentary on board cards or possibilities is generally annoying.
  • Don't discuss your hand until after the hand has been completed. If someone is trying to represent that they are holding 7/6, it becomes much more difficult to do so when you announce that you folded it.
  • Don't rabbit-hunt excessively (i.e., looking at what the fourth or fifth street would have been).
  • Once eliminated, it is courteous if that player acts as the dealer until another player is eliminated.
  • No mobile devices at the table, except the one displaying the poker clock. Leave the table if you need to check your messages.
  • Be present when it’s your turn. Expect to be auto-folded if you are not at the table when action is on you.
  • Consecutively auto-folding (sitting out) is not appreciated. Expect to be eliminated from the tournament if you sit out for 20 minutes or more.
